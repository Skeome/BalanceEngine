Comprehensive Analytical Verification Workflow

To standardize natural product chemical databases, researchers need a systematic, reproducible workflow to identify botanical sources, verify chemical structures, and assign stereochemical configurations.
This step-by-step protocol guides human researchers from crude sample collection to verified, stereospecific digital records.

Step 1: Specimen Authentication and Processing Documentation
Confirm the accepted botanical binomial of the plant by cross-referencing its common or Pinyin name with authoritative databases like the World Flora Online (WFO) or the International Plant Names Index (IPNI).
Identify the exact part used (e.g., flowering spikes, leaves, or mature seeds).
Document any processing methods (Pao Zhi) applied to the sample, such as smoking, steaming, or charring.
These processes can alter the chemical composition and form specific structural artifacts.

Step 2: Extract Harvesting and High-Resolution Mass Spectrometry
Extract the compounds using appropriate solvents based on polarity: use hexane or supercritical carbon dioxide for volatile lipophilic terpenes, and methanol or ethanol-water mixtures for polar glycosides and organic acids.
Analyze the extract using high-resolution liquid chromatography-tandem mass spectrometry (LC-MS/MS, such as UPLC-QToF-MS/MS) or gas chromatography-mass spectrometry (GC-MS).
Identify the precursor ion and product ions to calculate the exact monoisotopic mass.
Ensure the measured mass is within a $\pm$ 5 ppm tolerance window of the theoretical exact mass.
Compare the fragmentation patterns and retention times against verified reference standards.

Step 3: Structural Data Harvesting
Search chemical structures in databases like PubChem, ChEBI, ChemSpider, and the LOTUS Natural Products Database using the compound's common name or CAS number.
Retrieve the following identifiers for each compound:
Standard IUPAC name
Canonical SMILES (representing flat structure without stereocenters)
Non-isomeric IUPAC InChI string
Non-isomeric 27-character InChIKey
PubChem Compound Identifier (CID)
ChEBI identifier (ID)
ChemSpider identifier (ID)

Step 4: Stereochemical Validation and Classification
Retrieve the Isomeric SMILES from PubChem or ChEBI.
Inspect the string for stereochemical descriptors, such as @ or @@ for tetrahedral chiral centers, and / or \ for double-bond cis/trans geometries.
Extract the standard stereospecific InChIKey and compare it against the non-stereospecific version.
Classify the stereochemical configuration of the compound's representation into one of three categories:
- fully stereospecified:
Every chiral center and double-bond geometry is explicitly defined in the structural string. This includes achiral molecules where all atoms and connections are fully defined without undefined stereocenters.
- partially specified:
At least one chiral center or double-bond geometry is defined, but others are left undefined or represented as a racemic mixture.
- stereochemistry omitted:
No stereocenters or geometric isomers are defined, leaving the structural string flat.

Step 5: Digital Logging and Database Curation
Log all gathered data into a standardized database or spreadsheet utilizing the fifteen-field schema.
For computational modeling or virtual screening, convert the structural files into standard formats like Structure-Data Files (SDF) using software such as BIOVIA Draw or Open Babel.
Ensure all chiral flags remain intact during file conversions.
Keep these libraries linked to accepted botanical taxonomies for future reference.
